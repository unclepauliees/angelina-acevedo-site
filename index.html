The Work

Path 2 Potential

As Co-Founder and CEO, Angelina has grown Path 2 Potential into one of New York's fastest-growing providers of ABA therapy for children with autism — and led its expansion into Florida. The vision has never wavered: every child deserves the opportunity to reach their fullest potential, and every family deserves access to high-quality support and the resources to thrive.

The Approach

Servant leadership, by design.

Angelina leads on a simple belief: structure without compassion is cold, and compassion without structure doesn't scale. Her work sits at the intersection — building high-performing teams, durable operational systems, and a culture where care is the standard, not the exception. It's a leadership style built to outlast any single program.

01 · Build the system

Rigor that scales.

Operational systems that make great care repeatable — not dependent on any one person or moment.

02 · Protect the care

Quality families can feel.

Quality and accountability woven into every level of the organization — so families feel the difference from day one.

03 · Grow the people

Teams built to last.

A culture where clinicians are developed, supported, and proud of the work — and want to build their careers inside it.

About

Angelina Acevedo

For more than two decades, Angelina Acevedo has dedicated her career to building the systems, programs, and organizations that create meaningful opportunities for children and families. Before co-founding Path 2 Potential, she spent more than seven years with the Center for Autism and Related Disorders (CARD), one of the world's leading autism treatment organizations, where as Operations Manager she helped expand its New York presence and strengthen its service-delivery systems. Known for a servant-leadership style, she has built her career on the conviction that lasting change happens when compassion and structure work together — and she continues to lead with purpose, resilience, and vision.

"Success is not measured by the systems we build, but by the lives we help transform."

— Angelina Acevedo
